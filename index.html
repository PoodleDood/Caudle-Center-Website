Combining Traditional Methods with RF Energy

At The Caudle Center, we utilize cutting-edge liposuction techniques that go beyond traditional fat removal. By combining conventional liposuction with radiofrequency (RF) energy devices like BodyTite, we offer superior skin tightening and enhanced body shaping results.

Traditional Liposuction

Traditional liposuction involves the surgical removal of excess fat deposits to improve body contours. This time-tested technique uses a thin tube (cannula) to suction out fat cells through small incisions.

  • Ideal for: Removing larger volumes of fat
  • Benefits: Targeted precision for specific problem areas
  • Safety: Well-established safety profile
  • Results: Permanently removes fat cells

RF Energy Enhancement

Our RF energy enhancement uses advanced radiofrequency technology (BodyTite) to deliver targeted thermal energy to both the skin and underlying fat.

  • Skin Tightening: Up to 40% more skin contraction
  • Collagen Production: Stimulates natural collagen for firmer skin
  • Recovery: Reduces procedure and recovery time
  • Best For: Particularly effective for areas with skin laxity

Recovery Process

Our advanced techniques result in a more comfortable recovery with excellent results.

  • Return to Work: Most patients return within 3-7 days
  • Compression: Garments worn for 2-6 weeks
  • Bruising: Minimal compared to traditional methods
  • Final Results: Visible in 3-6 months as swelling resolves

Candidacy Requirements

The ideal candidate for our advanced liposuction procedures:

  • Weight: Within 30% of ideal body weight
  • Skin Quality: Good elasticity (RF helps with laxity)
  • Stability: Maintains a stable weight
  • Expectations: Has realistic goals about results
  • Health: Is in good overall health

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about liposuction procedures

Who is a good candidate for liposuction?

Suitable for patients in good health with realistic expectations and localized excess fat that resists diet and exercise. Ideal for those seeking a more youthful or contoured body shape.

Who is not a good candidate for liposuction?

Not recommended for individuals with serious or poorly controlled health issues, unrealistic expectations, excessive obesity, or those seeking absolute perfection.

Does liposuction produce permanent results?

Fat cells are permanently removed, ensuring lasting body contour improvements; however, weight gain in untreated areas may affect overall proportions.

How much fat can be removed?

Typically, 6 to 8 pounds (3 to 4 liters) of fat can be safely removed in one procedure; higher volumes may require multiple sessions spaced 3-4 weeks apart.

Does liposuction cause dimpling or indentations?

Minor dimpling is possible, but using microcannulas (less than 3mm) and a crisscross technique minimizes risks.

What alternatives exist to liposuction?

Alternatives include weight loss, nonsurgical body contouring, and tummy tuck procedures.

How soon will I see results?

Most patients observe 80-90% of final results within 1-3 months, with complete results evident after 6 months or more.

Will I be in pain?

The procedure can be done under general or tumescent (awake) anesthesia, with tumescent anesthesia providing profound local numbing and immediate communication of discomfort if needed.

How long does the procedure take?

The procedure itself typically takes 1-3 hours, with additional time required for the overall facility stay.

How much does liposuction cost?

Costs start at approximately $1500 for a single area; exact pricing depends on the number and location of areas treated, as determined in consultation.

What is the recovery like?

Recovery involves home care with rest, wearing a compression garment for several weeks, proper wound care, and a follow-up appointment within 3-7 days.

What are the risks?

Potential risks include swelling, bruising, minor lumps, dimpling, and rarely more significant complications such as scarring or infection. A detailed discussion during consultation is advised.

Lipedema Treatment

Specialized care for a misunderstood condition

Understanding Lipedema

Lipedema is a condition characterized by disproportionate fat accumulation in the arms, legs, and buttocks, often accompanied by pain, swelling, and joint discomfort. Unlike regular fat, lipedema fat cannot be lost through diet and exercise alone.

This condition can significantly impact quality of life, affecting both physical mobility and emotional well-being. Many patients experience self-esteem issues and daily discomfort that worsens over time.

How We Can Help

At The Caudle Center, we specialize in liposuction techniques specifically adapted for lipedema patients. Our approach can be crucial in alleviating both physical symptoms and emotional distress associated with this condition.

Through specialized lipocontouring, we can remove the painful lipedema fat and help restore normal body proportions, improving both comfort and confidence.
